What Does a Concrete Patio Cost in Logan?

As a rough guide, a standard broom-finish patio in the Cache Valley area runs around $8 to $15 per square foot installed, with decorative finishes costing more. The final price comes down to:

  • Size and shape — square footage and how many curves or angles are involved.
  • Finish — broom is most affordable; stamped, colored and exposed-aggregate cost more.
  • Site prep — grading, drainage and base work for your soil.
  • Access & tear-out — tight backyard access or removing an old slab adds labor.

Every yard is different, so the only way to get a real number is an on-site look. We give a clear, written estimate for free — no pressure.

Patio Finish & Design Options

  • Broom finish — the durable, slip-resistant, budget-friendly standard.
  • Stamped concrete — the look of flagstone, slate or pavers in one slab. See our stamped concrete page.
  • Exposed aggregate — textured, great traction, and hides wear well.
  • Colored / stained — integral color or stains in earth tones to match your home.
  • Decorative borders — banded edges that frame the space.

How We Build a Patio That Lasts

  1. Free on-site estimate — we measure, check drainage, and talk through finishes.
  2. Layout & slope — we set the grade so water always drains away from your home.
  3. Compacted gravel base — a solid, compacted sub-base so the slab won't settle.
  4. Forming & reinforcement — clean forms and rebar or mesh sized to the slab.
  5. Pour & finish — an air-entrained mix poured and finished in your chosen texture.
  6. Joints, cure & seal — control joints to manage cracking, proper curing, and a sealer to protect the surface.

Caring for Your Concrete Patio

Reseal every couple of years, sweep and rinse it occasionally, and avoid harsh deicing salts in the first winter, which can pit a fresh surface. With basic care, a concrete patio here easily lasts decades.

Patio FAQ

How much does a concrete patio cost in Logan?

A standard broom-finish patio generally runs about $8–$15 per square foot installed, with stamped or colored finishes costing more. Size, finish, site prep and access all affect the price. We give a free written estimate after seeing the space.

How thick should a patio be?

Four inches is standard for a residential patio. We add thickness and reinforcement for heavier uses like hot-tub pads. Proper base prep matters just as much as thickness in our freeze-thaw climate.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Logan?

A simple at-grade patio on private property often doesn't require a permit, but setbacks and covered structures can change that, and rules vary by city. We'll tell you what your specific project needs.

Concrete patio or pavers — which is better?

A poured concrete patio is one solid slab, so there are no joints for weeds to grow through and no individual pavers to shift or settle. It's generally lower-maintenance and easier to clean. Pavers can be lifted and replaced individually, but most homeowners here prefer the seamless, durable result of poured concrete.

Can you match my existing concrete?

We can usually get close on color and finish, but new concrete cures lighter and ages differently than old, so an exact match isn't guaranteed. For a seamless look, a fresh finish or a stain over the whole area often works best.

What's the best finish around a pool?

A textured, slip-resistant finish like broom or exposed aggregate is ideal around water, and a light color stays cooler underfoot. We'll recommend the right option for your pool deck.

How long does a concrete patio last?

With proper base prep, an air-entrained mix and occasional resealing, a concrete patio in Cache Valley commonly lasts 25–30 years or more.
