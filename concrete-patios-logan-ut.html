Outdoor Living That Lasts

A concrete patio is one of the highest-value additions you can make to a Cache Valley home — durable, low-maintenance, and far more affordable than pavers or natural stone. But a patio takes the same freeze-thaw beating a driveway does, so the prep matters: a compacted base, the right thickness, proper drainage away from your foundation, and control joints placed so the slab cracks where it should.

We build patios, walkways and sidewalks throughout Logan, North Logan, Smithfield, Providence, Hyrum, Nibley and the rest of Cache Valley.

  • Smooth / troweled — a clean modern look for covered areas.
  • Stamped & stained — the look of stone or slate in one solid slab.
  • Exposed aggregate — textured and durable, ideal for traction.

Patio FAQ

How much does a concrete patio cost in Logan?

It depends on square footage, thickness and finish — a broom-finish patio costs less than a stamped or colored one. We give you a clear written estimate after seeing your yard, so there's no guessing.

How thick should my patio be?

Four inches over a compacted base is standard for a residential patio. Drainage and control joints matter just as much as thickness for handling freeze-thaw.

Can you pour right against my house?

Yes — we slope the slab so water runs away from your foundation and use an expansion joint where the patio meets the house so they move independently.
