Concrete Foundations, Footings & Flatwork in Cache Valley

The foundation is the most important concrete on any property — everything else sits on top of it. It has to be level, square, properly reinforced, and built for Utah's frost depth and soils, or the whole structure pays for it later. We pour footings, foundation walls and structural flatwork for homes, garages, shops and additions throughout Logan, North Logan, Smithfield, Providence, Hyrum, Nibley and across Cache Valley.

Why Proper Foundation Work Matters Here

Cache Valley's cold winters and variable soils make foundation details critical:

  • Frost depth — footings must sit below the frost line so they don't heave and crack when the ground freezes.
  • Soil & compaction — the ground has to be evaluated and compacted so the foundation doesn't settle unevenly.
  • Reinforcement — properly placed rebar ties the foundation together and resists cracking.
  • Drainage — grading and drainage keep water away from the footings and walls.

Get these right and the structure stands square and solid for generations.

What Does a Concrete Foundation Cost?

Foundation cost varies widely with the type and size of the project — a simple shed slab is a very different job from a full basement foundation. The main factors are square footage, wall height and thickness, footing depth, reinforcement, excavation and the amount of site prep your soil requires. Because of that range, the right way to price a foundation is an on-site evaluation. We provide a free, detailed estimate or bid for your specific plans.

Our Foundation Process

  1. Site evaluation & layout — we review plans, check soil and drainage, and stake the layout square.
  2. Excavation — we dig footings and the foundation area to the correct depth.
  3. Footings — poured below the frost line with rebar to spec.
  4. Forming & reinforcement — we form the walls and place rebar per the plans and code.
  5. Pour & finish — walls and slabs poured with the correct mix and finished flat and level.
  6. Strip, cure & backfill — forms stripped, concrete cured to strength, and the site backfilled and graded.

We coordinate with your general contractor and the required inspections so the foundation passes and your build stays on schedule.

Foundations FAQ

How deep do footings need to be in Utah?

Footings must extend below the local frost line so the ground freezing and thawing can't heave them. In the Cache Valley area that's typically around 30 inches, but we build to the depth your local code and plans require.

How much does a foundation cost?

It depends heavily on the type and size — a shed slab versus a full basement are very different jobs. Square footage, wall height, footing depth, reinforcement and excavation all factor in. We give a free, detailed estimate for your specific plans.

How long before we can build on it?

Concrete reaches enough strength to build on within several days to a week depending on conditions, and full strength at about 28 days. We'll advise on timing for your specific project and inspections.

Do you handle permits and inspections?

We build to code and coordinate with your general contractor and the required inspections so the footings and foundation pass. Permitting is usually pulled by the GC or owner, and we'll work within it.

How thick should a garage or shop slab be?

A standard garage slab is typically 4 inches; shop floors and slabs that carry heavy equipment, lifts or RVs get more thickness and reinforcement. We size the slab to how you'll use it.

My foundation has cracks — can that be fixed?

Some cracking is normal; structural or widening cracks and settling need evaluation. Repair or leveling is often possible — see our concrete repair page, and we'll give you an honest assessment.

Footings & Frost Depth: Why It Matters in Northern Utah

The single most important thing about a foundation in our climate is that footings have to sit below the frost line. When the ground freezes and thaws, soil heaves — and a footing placed too shallow gets lifted and dropped season after season, cracking the structure above it. Cache Valley winters are cold enough that footings need to go well deeper than they would in a mild climate, and they're built to local code for exactly this reason.

Just as important is what's underneath and around the concrete: a properly compacted base, drainage that moves water away from the foundation, a vapor barrier under interior slabs, and rebar sized to the load. Skip those steps and you get the settling and cracking we get called to repair later. We do them right the first time.
