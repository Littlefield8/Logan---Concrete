High-End Looks Without the High-End Price

Decorative concrete lets you get the appearance of pavers, flagstone, brick or wood plank for a fraction of the cost — and because it's poured as one continuous slab, there are no joints for weeds to invade or individual pieces to shift and heave over a Cache Valley winter. The key is doing it right: solid base prep, the correct mix, even color, and a quality sealer that stands up to our freeze-thaw cycles and road salt.

We install decorative concrete throughout Logan, North Logan, Smithfield, Providence, Hyrum, Nibley and across Cache Valley.

Decorative Options

  • Stamped concrete — stone, slate, brick, cobble and wood-plank patterns.
  • Integral & broadcast color — earth tones blended through or onto the slab.
  • Acid & water-based stains — rich, variegated color on new or existing concrete.
  • Exposed aggregate — natural stone texture with great traction.
  • Stampable overlays — a decorative surface over a sound existing slab.

Decorative Concrete FAQ

Is stamped concrete cheaper than pavers?

Usually, yes — you get the paver or stone look at a lower installed cost, with no joints for weeds or shifting pieces because it's one solid slab.

Will it survive our winters?

Yes, when it's built right: proper base, air-entrained concrete and a good sealer. We recommend resealing every couple of years to protect the color and surface against freeze-thaw and salt.

Can you stamp my existing slab?

Stamping happens in fresh concrete, but a sound existing slab can often take a stampable or stained overlay for a similar look without a full tear-out.
