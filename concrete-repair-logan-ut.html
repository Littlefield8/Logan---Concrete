Concrete Repair & Leveling in Logan & Cache Valley

Cracked, sunken or flaking concrete is one of the most common things we see after a few Cache Valley winters — and the good news is that it's often repairable for a fraction of the cost of a full replacement. We repair and level driveways, patios, sidewalks, garage floors and steps throughout Logan, North Logan, Smithfield, Providence, Hyrum, Nibley and across Cache Valley.

Repair Services We Offer

  • Concrete leveling — raising sunken slabs back into place (slabjacking / foam jacking).
  • Crack repair — cleaning, filling and sealing cracks before they spread.
  • Resurfacing — a fresh wear surface over sound but worn concrete.
  • Spall repair — fixing flaked, pitted surfaces.
  • Trip-hazard grinding — leveling raised joints and edges.
  • Joint & crack sealing — keeping water out so winter can't make damage worse.

Repair or Replace?

We'll always give you the honest answer. Repair usually makes sense when a slab has sunk or settled but is otherwise solid, or when cracks are surface-level and not spreading. Replacement is the better investment when concrete is crumbling, has deep structural cracks, or sits on a failed base. If your slab needs replacing, see our driveway and patio pages — but whenever a repair will genuinely solve the problem, we'll tell you, because it saves you money.

What Causes Concrete to Fail in Cache Valley

  • Freeze-thaw cycles — water gets into cracks and pores, freezes, expands, and breaks the concrete apart.
  • Poor base / settling — concrete poured on uncompacted soil sinks and cracks.
  • Water & drainage — runoff that washes out the base under a slab.
  • Deicing salts — accelerate surface spalling, especially on younger concrete.
  • Tree roots — lift and crack nearby slabs.

What Does Concrete Repair Cost?

Repair almost always costs a fraction of tear-out and replacement, which is exactly why it's worth getting evaluated first. Leveling a sunken slab is typically far cheaper than pouring a new one, and crack or spall repair is cheaper still. Cost depends on the size of the area and the type of fix. We provide a free assessment and written estimate.

How Concrete Leveling Works

  1. Assess — we determine why the slab sank and whether leveling is the right fix.
  2. Drill — small, discreet holes are drilled in the sunken slab.
  3. Lift — we pump material beneath the slab to fill voids and raise it back to level.
  4. Patch — the holes are patched, and the slab is ready to use, often the same day.

It's faster, cleaner and less expensive than tear-out — and there's no curing wait like a new pour.

Repair FAQ

Is leveling cheaper than replacing?

Almost always. Raising and leveling a sunken slab typically costs a fraction of removing and pouring a new one — which is why we recommend an assessment before assuming you need a replacement.

Can a sunken slab really be raised?

Yes. If the slab itself is still solid, we can pump material underneath to fill the voids and lift it back to level, rather than tearing it out.

How long does concrete leveling last?

When the underlying cause is addressed, a properly leveled slab can last for years. Modern foam materials are stable and water-resistant. We'll explain what to expect for your specific slab.

Can you fix cracks permanently?

We clean, fill and seal cracks to stop water intrusion and slow their spread. No crack repair is truly forever, but addressing it early prevents the freeze-thaw damage that turns a small crack into a replacement.

Can flaking or pitted concrete (spalling) be fixed?

If the damage is on the surface and the slab is structurally sound, resurfacing can restore it. If spalling is deep or widespread, replacement may be the better value — we'll assess it honestly.

How long does a repair take?

Many repairs, including slab leveling, are completed in a single day with no long curing wait, so you can use the area again quickly.

Can you save my driveway, or do I need a new one?

It depends on the damage. Surface issues and settling are often repairable; a slab that's crumbling or badly cracked throughout is usually better replaced. We'll give you a straight answer either way.

Mudjacking vs. Polyurethane Foam Leveling

There are two main ways to raise a sunken slab, and both beat tearing it out. Mudjacking (also called slabjacking) pumps a cement-based slurry under the concrete through small holes to lift it back into place — it's proven, economical, and great for driveways, sidewalks and patios. Polyurethane foam leveling injects an expanding foam instead; it's lighter, cures in minutes, and uses smaller holes, which can be ideal where weight or speed matters.

Both are far cheaper and faster than replacement and let you use the surface again quickly. At your free estimate we'll look at the slab, the cause of the settling, and the access, then recommend the method that gives you the best long-term result for the lowest cost.
