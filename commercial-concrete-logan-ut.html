Commercial Concrete Contractor in Logan & Cache Valley

Commercial concrete has to do more than look good — it has to carry heavy loads, meet code and ADA requirements, and hold up to constant traffic and weather. We handle commercial flatwork and structural concrete for business owners, property managers and general contractors throughout Logan, North Logan, Smithfield, Providence, Hyrum, Nibley and across Cache Valley.

Commercial Services We Offer

  • Parking lots & drive lanes — heavy-duty slabs built for traffic loads.
  • Sidewalks & walkways — including ADA-compliant routes.
  • ADA ramps & truncated domes — built to accessibility standards.
  • Approaches, aprons & curb and gutter.
  • Loading docks & dumpster pads — reinforced for heavy, repeated loads.
  • Equipment & mechanical pads.
  • Foundations, footings & warehouse/shop slabs.

What Sets Commercial Concrete Apart

  • Load ratings & thickness — slabs engineered for the vehicles and equipment they'll carry.
  • Reinforcement — heavier rebar and proper jointing for high-traffic durability.
  • ADA compliance — ramps, slopes and detectable warnings built to spec.
  • Scheduling — we phase work to keep your business open and minimize disruption.
  • Drainage & grading — engineered so water moves where it should.

What Does Commercial Concrete Cost?

Commercial pricing depends entirely on scope and specs — square footage, slab thickness and load rating, reinforcement, site prep, drainage and ADA requirements all factor in. For general contractors and property managers, we provide detailed bids you can build into your project budget. For business owners, we give a free, clear estimate. Either way, the right number comes from reviewing the plans or walking the site.

Our Commercial Process

  1. Site evaluation & bid — we review plans or walk the site and provide a detailed bid.
  2. Prep & excavation — demolition, grading and a compacted base built to the load spec.
  3. Forming & reinforcement — forms set to grade with rebar sized for commercial loads.
  4. Pour & finish — the correct mix poured, finished and jointed for durability.
  5. Cure & clean-up — proper curing and a clean, ready-to-use site.

Who We Work With

We partner with business owners, property managers, HOAs, developers and general contractors across Cache Valley — from a single dumpster pad or ADA ramp to a full parking lot or building foundation. We're comfortable coordinating with your GC, schedule and inspections.

Commercial Concrete FAQ

Do you bid general contractor projects?

Yes. We provide detailed, itemized bids for GCs and property managers and coordinate with your schedule, specs and inspections.

Do you handle ADA-compliant work?

Yes — ADA ramps, slopes, landings and truncated-dome detectable warnings built to current accessibility standards.

Can you work after hours or in phases to avoid disrupting my business?

We routinely phase commercial work and can schedule around your operating hours so customers and staff keep access while we pour section by section.

How thick is a commercial parking lot slab?

Thicker and more heavily reinforced than residential — the exact thickness depends on the expected traffic and loads, which we spec to the project.

Do you do commercial tear-out and replacement?

Yes. We handle demolition, haul-away and repour of failed commercial flatwork, including parking lots, sidewalks and pads.

Do you pull permits and meet inspections?

We build to code and coordinate with the required inspections. Permitting is typically handled by the owner or GC, and we work within it to keep the project moving.

Minimizing Downtime for Your Business

For a business, concrete work isn't just about the slab — it's about staying open while it gets done. We plan commercial projects around your operations: pouring in phases so part of a parking lot or sidewalk stays usable, scheduling after-hours or weekend work where it makes sense, and sequencing the job so customers and deliveries can still get in and out.

We also handle the details that keep a commercial project compliant and durable — proper slab thickness and reinforcement for heavy loads and traffic, ADA-compliant ramps and sidewalk slopes, and clean transitions to existing pavement. Tell us your hours and constraints at the bid stage and we'll build the schedule around them.
